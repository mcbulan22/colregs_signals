Rule 34(a) — Manoeuvring Signals (vessels in sight)

• one short
I am altering my course to starboard
• • two short
I am altering my course to port
• • • three short
I am operating astern propulsion (does not necessarily mean the vessel is moving astern)

Rule 34(b) — Supplementary Light Signals

1 flash
Altering course to starboard
2 flashes
Altering course to port
3 flashes
Operating astern propulsion
Timing
Each flash ≈ 1 s; interval between flashes ≈ 1 s; between successive signals ≥ 10 s. All-round white light, visible ≥ 5 miles.

Rule 34(c) — Overtaking in Narrow Channel or Fairway

— — •
I intend to overtake you on your starboard side
— — • •
I intend to overtake you on your port side
— • — •
Agreement signal from vessel being overtaken (prolong-short-prolong-short)

Rule 34(d) — Doubt / Danger Signal

≥ 5 short rapid
Either vessel in doubt about intentions or whether sufficient action is being taken to avoid collision. May be supplemented by ≥ 5 rapid flashes.

Rule 34(e) — Blind Bend

— one prolonged
Vessel nearing a bend where others may be obscured. Any approaching vessel within hearing replies with one prolonged blast.

Rule 34(f) — Multiple Whistles

One whistle only
If whistles are fitted more than 100 m apart, only one shall be used for manoeuvring and warning signals.
